Plato Tipico Rolo

Odin Recipes

Description

Ajiaco is a beloved traditional soup from Bogotá, Colombia, cherished for its comforting warmth and rich, layered flavors. This iconic dish is made with three varieties of potatoes—criolla (small and yellow), sabanera (red-skinned), and pastusa (white)—which each contribute different textures and thickness to the broth. The soup is simmered with bone-in chicken, fresh corn on the cob, and a native Colombian herb called guasca, which gives ajiaco its signature earthy taste. Typically served in deep bowls, ajiaco is accompanied by white rice, sliced avocado, capers, and a dollop of crema de leche (Colombian-style cream), allowing each person to customize their bowl. It’s more than just a meal; ajiaco is a symbol of Colombian heritage and hospitality, often enjoyed during family gatherings or holidays.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (bone-in, skinless)
  • 3 ears of fresh corn, cut into halves
  • 3 medium russet (pastusa) potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 3 red (sabanera) potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 6 small yellow (criolla) potatoes, whole and unpeeled
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 bunch of fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon dried guasca (Colombian herb)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Capers, avocado slices, and crema de leche (for serving)
  • Cooked white rice (optional, served on the side)

Cooking Steps

  1. Place the chicken breasts in a large pot with about 10 cups of water. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30–40 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  2. Remove the chicken from the pot, shred it into bite-sized pieces, and set it aside. Leave the broth in the pot.
  3. Add the sliced russet and red potatoes to the broth and cook for about 15 minutes until they begin to soften.
  4. Add the whole yellow (criolla) potatoes and the corn halves to the pot. Continue to cook until all potatoes are tender and start to thicken the soup—about 20 more minutes.
  5. Stir in the guasca and let the soup simmer for another 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  6. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  7. To serve, ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with capers, a spoonful of crema de leche, and avocado slices. Serve with a side of white rice if desired.
