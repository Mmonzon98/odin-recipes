Plato Tipico Tolimense

Description

The Tamal Tolimense is a beloved traditional dish from Colombia’s Tolima region, known for its rich flavor and comforting ingredients. Wrapped in plantain leaves and steamed, this tamal is made with a seasoned corn dough (masa) filled with a savory mix of pork, chicken, rice, peas, carrots, potatoes, and hard-boiled eggs. The slow cooking process infuses the ingredients with deep, hearty flavor, making it a popular choice for breakfast or special occasions, often enjoyed with a hot cup of chocolate or coffee.

Ingridients

  • Corn dough (masa)
  • Pork (chunks)
  • Chicken (pieces, bone-in)
  • Rice
  • Carrots (sliced)
  • Potatoes (sliced)
  • Green peas
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Garlic, onions, and spices (for seasoning)
  • Plantain leaves (for wrapping)

Cooking Steps

  1. Prepare the filling: Season and cook the pork and chicken with garlic, onions, and spices until tender.
  2. Cook the rice slightly: Parboil the rice so it's partially cooked.
  3. Make the masa: Mix corn flour with broth and seasonings to form a soft dough.
  4. Prep the leaves: Soften plantain leaves by passing them over heat or boiling briefly.
  5. Assemble the tamal: On a leaf, spread masa, then layer meat, rice, peas, carrots, potatoes, and a slice of hard-boiled egg.
  6. Wrap and tie: Fold the leaf to enclose the filling and tie with kitchen string.
  7. Steam: Place wrapped tamales in a large pot with water and steam for 2–3 hours until fully cooked.
  8. Serve hot: Usually enjoyed with hot chocolate or coffee.
